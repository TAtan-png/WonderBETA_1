HEALTH INFORMATION

CDC recommends the flu vaccine for everyone 6 months of age and older, in addition to staying up-to-date on COVID-19 vaccinations. Depending on the destination, some regions may also require specific vaccines before travel. Changes in circadian rhythm (e.g., jet lag) and lack of sleep can trigger seizures in people with epilepsy, cause migraine attacks, and cause more behavioral symptoms in people with dementia. Everyone planning to travel should seek information or advice about potential health risks before traveling. Travelers may encounter a variety of health risks while traveling.
These risks can depend on many factors, including the traveler's health, the type of trip, and the destination. Health risks can arise from factors such as changes in temperature and humidity, air pollution, safety issues, access to health and dental care services and products, exposure to infectious diseases, access to safe food and water, sanitation and hygiene standards, availability of facilities and equipment for disabled people, and local laws and customs.

DANGEROUS ANIMALS
